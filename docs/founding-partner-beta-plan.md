# PhotoBrief.ai — Founding Partner Beta Plan

---

## Beta Objective

Validate that PhotoBrief solves a real, recurring photo collection problem for businesses — well enough that they'd pay for it after the beta ends.

Specifically:

1. Confirm the core loop works: business sends request → customer submits photos → business gets organized, AI-reviewed media back
2. Identify which workflows and segments get the most value
3. Establish baseline usage and retention metrics before launch pricing
4. Collect testimonials and case studies from real usage

This is not a "get feedback on a prototype" beta. Partners use PhotoBrief on real jobs with real customers.

---

## Target Customer Segments

Businesses that regularly need photos from people who aren't in the room — customers, field teams, tenants, contractors, claimants.

| Segment | Example workflow | Why they hurt |
|---|---|---|
| **Roofing / exteriors** | Storm damage documentation, pre/post install photos | Chase customers over text, get blurry unusable shots |
| **Property management** | Move-in/move-out condition reports, maintenance requests | Tenants send random photos with no context or consistency |
| **Insurance / claims** | Damage documentation from policyholders | Incomplete submissions delay processing, require follow-up |
| **Home services / HVAC** | Pre-visit site photos, equipment condition | Techs or customers send photos to personal phones, nothing organized |
| **Ecommerce returns** | Product condition photos from customers | No structured way to collect visual evidence for returns |
| **Equipment / fleet** | Inspection checklists with photo evidence | Paper forms or scattered phone photos, no audit trail |

**Priority segments for beta:** Roofing, property management, home services. These have the highest photo volume and the most pain with current methods.

## Qualification Criteria

Score each applicant 1–5 on fit. Accept 4+ automatically, review 3s, decline 1–2s.

| Factor | Strong fit (4–5) | Weak fit (1–2) |
|---|---|---|
| **Photo volume** | 10+ photo requests/month | "Maybe a few per quarter" |
| **Current pain** | Actively chasing people for photos, losing time | "It's fine, we just use email" |
| **Workflow clarity** | Can describe exactly when/why they need photos | Vague or aspirational use case |
| **Decision authority** | Owner or ops lead who can implement | "I'd need to check with my team" |
| **Tech comfort** | Uses software tools daily, not paper-first | Resistant to new tools |
| **Segment fit** | Roofing, property mgmt, insurance, home services | Consumer/personal use, photography studio |

**Hard disqualifiers:**
- Personal use (not a business)
- Photography/creative studios (they produce photos, not collect them)
- No recurring need (one-off project)
- Can't articulate current workflow

## Beta Offer

**Duration:** 60 days (the clock starts 2 weeks after the final seat is filled, giving every partner time for concierge setup)
**Cohort size:** 30 companies total — 5 accepted per week across 6 weekly cohorts

What founding partners get:

- **Free access** for the full 60-day beta period
- **Hands-on onboarding** — we configure their first template with them
- **Direct line** to the team via email and in-app feedback
- **Tiered founding partner rewards** based on quality of feedback (see below)
- **Early access** to every new feature during and after beta
- **Co-marketing opportunity** — optional case study or testimonial feature at launch

Rewards are granted based on quality of feedback — depth, consistency, and actionability — not just usage volume.

| Tier | Partners | Reward |

**What drives tier placement:**

What we do NOT offer:

## Beta Phases

30 companies total, 5 accepted per week. The 60-day beta clock starts 2 weeks after the final (30th) seat is filled, so all partners begin and end together.

### Phase 1: Seed (Weeks 1–2)
- **Goal:** First 10 partners onboarded and sending real requests (cohorts 1–2)
- Accept 5 partners per week from application pool
- Hands-on async onboarding for each partner (configure first template via chat/email, walk through first request)
- Daily monitoring of every request and submission
- Fix critical bugs same-day

### Phase 2: Validate (Weeks 3–5)
- **Goal:** Confirm core loop retention — partners who send requests keep sending them
- Continue accepting 5 partners per week (cohorts 3–5, reaching 25 total)
- Track request-to-submission completion rate
- Two-week async feedback check-in with every partner (chat, email, or in-app)
- Identify top 3 feature gaps from real usage
- Begin nudging stalled partners or closing out poor-fit accounts

### Phase 3: Optimize (Weeks 6–7)
- **Goal:** Improve activation rate and submission quality based on data
- Accept final cohort (cohort 6, reaching 30 total)
- Ship highest-impact feature improvements
- Collect testimonials from active partners
- Run pricing validation conversations
- Begin preliminary tier assessments based on feedback quality

### Phase 4: Graduate (Weeks 8–9)
- **Goal:** Transition beta partners to paid plans with tiered rewards
- Finalize reward tier placement for each partner
- Send personalized graduation emails with usage stats and tier-specific pricing
- Close beta applications
- Sunset free access on a specific date
- Publish case studies and testimonials

## Onboarding Flow

For each accepted partner:

1. **Accept + invite email** — `founding-partner-welcome` template with account creation link
2. **Account creation** — partner signs up, workspace is auto-provisioned with beta flags
3. **First template setup** (async via chat or email)
   - Review their workflow: what photos they need, from whom, when
   - Configure a template that matches their actual process
   - If they have a common scenario (e.g., "roof inspection"), use a pre-built starter
4. **First request** — walk them through sending their first real request (not a test)
5. **First submission review** — check in after their first customer submits photos
6. **Nudge at day 3** if no requests sent — `beta-first-request-nudge` email

**Target time from acceptance to first real request sent: < 48 hours**

## Success Metrics

### Activation
- **Target:** 80% of accepted partners send their first request within 48 hours
- **Target:** 60% of accepted partners send 5+ requests in first two weeks

### Retention
- **Target:** 50% of activated partners are still sending requests in week 6
- **Target:** Weekly request volume per active partner: 3+

### Completion
- **Target:** 70% of sent requests result in a completed submission
- **Target:** Average submission time (customer receives link → submits): < 24 hours

### Quality
- **Target:** 80% of beta feedback responses rate briefs as "useful"
- **Target:** < 20% of submissions require follow-up for missing/unusable photos

### Conversion
- **Target:** 25 of 30 partners convert to paid at graduation (across all reward tiers)
- **Target:** Average founding partner MRR: $50+ (after tier-specific discount)

### Leading indicators (watch weekly)
- Requests created this week (total and per-partner)
- Submission completion rate
- Time from request sent to submission completed
- Feedback card responses (useful vs. not useful)
- Partner reply rate to check-in emails

## Exit Criteria

**Beta is successful if:**
- At least 20 of 30 partners actively used PhotoBrief on real workflows for 4+ weeks
- Submission completion rate ≥ 60%
- At least 15 partners say they'd pay for it (validated in pricing conversations)
- At least 5 usable testimonials collected
- No category of critical bug unresolved

**Beta should be extended if:**
- Activation rate < 50% (onboarding needs work, not the product)
- Completion rate < 40% (customer-facing experience needs improvement)
- Fewer than 15 active partners after 5 weeks

**Beta should be stopped/pivoted if:**
- Partners consistently say "this is nice but I wouldn't pay for it"
- Submission completion rate < 30% after fixes
- No segment shows strong retention after 5 weeks

## Pricing Validation Questions

Ask these during check-in conversations (weeks 5–8), not via survey:

1. **Value anchor:** "If you didn't have PhotoBrief, how would you handle this? How much time does that take per job?"
2. **Willingness to pay:** "If this stayed exactly as it is today, would you pay for it? What feels like a fair monthly price?"
3. **Volume sensitivity:** "How many photo requests do you send in a typical month? Would a per-request price or a flat monthly fee work better for you?"
4. **Comparison:** "Have you looked at other tools for this? What did you think of them?"
5. **Deal-breaker:** "Is there one thing that, if we don't add it, would stop you from paying?"
6. **Upgrade trigger:** "What would make you want a higher-tier plan? More team members? More requests? Integrations?"

**Do not:**
- Send a pricing survey — these conversations need to be 1:1
- Show a pricing page during beta — it anchors expectations before we have data
- Promise specific pricing — say "founding partner discount" and leave the exact number for graduation

## Decision Rules for What to Build During Beta

### Build it now (during beta)
- **It blocks activation:** Partners can't complete onboarding or send their first request
- **It breaks the core loop:** Request → submission → review flow is broken or confusing
- **Multiple partners hit the same wall:** If 3+ partners independently report the same friction, it's real
- **It takes < 1 day to ship:** Small improvements that reduce support load

### Build it next (queue for post-beta)
- **One partner wants it:** Interesting but not validated across the cohort
- **It's an optimization, not a fix:** "It works, but it could be faster/prettier"
- **It requires new infrastructure:** Storage changes, new integrations, billing logic
- **It's a segment-specific feature:** Only matters for one vertical (e.g., insurance-specific fields)

### Don't build it
- **It's a "nice to have" with no usage signal:** No partner has asked, no data suggests it matters
- **It adds complexity without clarity:** "Maybe we should add X?" — if you can't tie it to a specific partner's workflow, skip it
- **It's premature optimization:** Performance, scale, and polish can wait until we know the product is right
- **A partner asked for it once and never mentioned it again:** Requests that don't repeat aren't priorities

### Process
1. Log every feature request with the partner name and context in the admin notes
2. Weekly: review requests, tag recurring themes
3. If 3+ partners request the same thing → move to "build it now" or "build it next"
4. If 1 partner requests it → acknowledge, log, don't commit
5. Ship one meaningful improvement per week during beta — no more, no less
